Two-group Confidence Intervals and Hypothesis Tests

Compare VC vs OJ effects at different dose levels

There are three dose levels (0.5, 1.0 and 2.0 mg).
1. dose is 0.5 mg

## [1] "The variance of VC 7.544 is different from the variance of OJ 19.889, therefore the T-test will use the option of va.equal=FALSE"

The 95% confidence interval is [1.719, 8.781], which means “OJ” is more effective than “VC” on length of odontoblasts.

  1. dose is 1.0 mg
## [1] "The variance of VC 6.327 is different from the variance of OJ 15.296, therefore the T-test will use the option of va.equal=FALSE"
##          [,1]     [,2]
## [1,] 2.802148 9.057852

The 95% confidence interval is [2.802, 9.058], which means “OJ” is more effective than “VC” on length of odontoblasts.

  1. dose is 2.0 mg
## [1] "The variance of VC 23.018 is different from the variance of OJ 7.049, therefore the T-test will use the option of va.equal=FALSE"

The 95% confidence interval is [-3.798, 3.638], which means “OJ” is not different from “VC” on length of odontoblasts.

Compare dose effects (0.5 and 2.0 mg) at VC or OJ

There are three dose levels (0.5, 1.0 and 2.0 mg).
1. supp is VC

## [1] "The variance of 0.5 mg dose 7.544 is different from the variance of 2.0 mg dose 23.018, therefore the T-test will use the option of va.equal=FALSE"

The 95% confidence interval is [14.418, 21.902], which means 2.0 mg dose is not effective than 0.5 mg dose on length of odontoblasts in the group of VC.

  1. supp is OJ
## [1] "The variance of 0.5 mg dose 19.889 is different from the variance of 2.0 mg dose 7.049, therefore the T-test will use the option of va.equal=FALSE"

The 95% confidence interval is [9.325, 16.335], which means 2.0 mg dose is not effective than 0.5 mg dose on length of odontoblasts in the group of OJ.
